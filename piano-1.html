Getting Familiar with the Notes


Notes are the building blocks of music. Those notes are seen on sheet music, but how do you understand what the notes mean? First let's take a look at a simple section of music.

The area above is called a staff. In this example, we have the staff split into 2 measures. Each measure contains 4 beats. The first 4 notes seen here are called quarter notes, since each note corresponds to a single beat. In the 2nd measure, the first two notes are eighth notes. Eighth notes are half a beat long. As you can see, 2 eighth notes = 1 quarter note.

Now that we can determine the type of note, we will look at how they are placed on the staff. Each line and space on the staff corresponds to a note. The lines, beginning from the bottom can easily be remembered as Every Good Boy Does Fine. That is E - G - B - D - F. The spaces in the treble clef can be remembered as FACE, or F - A - C - E.

The bass clef is a little different. The spaces are A - C - E - G, or All Cars Eat Gas and the lines are G - B - D - F - A, or Good Burritos Don't Fall Apart.

All notes have a letter assigned to them. They range from A - G, repeating over and over. For example:

The first thing you should notice is the layout of the keys. They are layed out in a patter of 7 white keys and 5 black keys. This pattern repeats along the entire length of the piano. From the beginning of one set of keys and ending at the start of the next set, an interval is formed. Intervals are just the musical distances between two sounds. This particular interval is called an octave. (See below)


Octave from C to C

You may be wondering what the black keys are since all the white keys are labeled with their note letter. The black keys can be either sharp(♯) or flat(♭). We will use the 'D' note for this example. The black key directly to the right of the 'D' key would be called D♯ and the black key directly to the left of the 'D' key is D♭. Since D♯ is to the left of the 'E' key, it is also E♭. Don't worry if this seems a little confusing, as you play and learn more, it will become easier to identify the keys.
