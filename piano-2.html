Clefs


In the diagram above, The symbols that are seen are found on the staff, usually at the beginning. On a piano, it is more common to see just the first two, The Treble Clef and the Bass Clef. The Treble Clef, which is also called the G Clef, contains the notes for the right hand. The staff line the clef wraps around is known as G. The Bass Clef, also called the F Clef contains the notes to be played by the left hand. The staff line between the two dots of the clef is F.

Chords


Chords are the sounds you get when you combine 3 or more individual notes. For the examples shown here, we will be using the C chord. Basic chords consist of a root, third and fifth note. For this chord, C is the Root note. If we take 2 whole steps (White Key → Black Key = one-half step and White Key → White Key = Whole Step), we land on the E key. Next, we take 2 more whole steps to arrive at the fifth, or G note.

C + E + G = C Major Chord
D + F♯ + A = D Major Chord
