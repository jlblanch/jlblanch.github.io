Key Signatures

Key signatures are similar to scales with the exception that the key signature is noted at the beginning. For example: if you look at the staff below, you will notice the sharps and flats.

Each set of sharps and flats corresponds to a specific key. Select the G Major scale from the selector and count the number of sharps or flats in the scale. You will notice that there is only 1 sharp(F♯). On the staff above, it is the 2nd group. If you select the E Minor scale, you will notice it is exactly the same as G Major. Each Major scale has an equivalent minor scale. Don't worry if this seems confusing, as you play around with the scales and you will see the pattern.

Chord Progression

Chord Progressions can be a little confusing. In any song you hear, there is a particular pattern that the song follows. This pattern is called a chord progression. Most commonly, musicians will use what is called a I - IV - V progression. These chords have a tendency to naturally sound good together. There are literally hundreds of variations that can be accomplished. It's all a matter of what you, the musician, likes. The diagrams below outline the most common chord progressions.
